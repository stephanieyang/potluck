Why We Need Potluck

With millions of households across the world struggling to have enough to eat, and millions of tons of food being tossed in the garbage, food waste is being seen as a serious environmental and economic issue. This is a problem not limited to the United States. About 60 million metric tons of food is wasted a year in the United States, which are worth $165 billion. Out of the 60 million metric tons wasted, 32 million end up in municipal landfills, which cost local governments about $1.5 billion a year.

Third of all the food produced in the world is never consumed, but reducing food waste from 20 to 50 percent globally could save $120 billion to $300 billion a year by 2030.

How Potluck Works

Due to the variation in quality of sell-uploaded goods, sellers will be required to upload the purchase date, a desired price, an expiration date, and a picture. Sellers will then be required to categorize their listing into broad categories such as Meat, Vegetables, Microwaveables, and Snacks. Listings will self-destruct at the end of the expiration day. Sellers will be pushed notifications close to the expiration date offering them price-cutting options.

Buyers can search items and ask questions to the sellers through commenting. Meet-up locations and payment methods can be negotiated between users.
